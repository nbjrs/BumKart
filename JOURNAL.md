# May 24: GoKart Cad started! GALVANIZED SQUARE STEEL!!!

AND ECO FRIENDLY WOOD VENEERS (not). Just realized I am using galvanized square steel for the frame which didn't click before and now that it did I find it so funny. The main part of this CAD session was the start of the actual gokart CAD. I went over all of my resources and worked based off of the following todo list I made to start.

1)Import engine cad
2)Create a part studio for customisable square beams of diff size
3)Get an idea of overall shape, will I keep it a square or not?
4)Get all ordering parts that are important (wheels/others) into the cad(either designing or importing)
5)Start making mounting for the steering support beam for both sides (decide what filament later)

(Added later at the end)
6)Make custom corner magnets for welding???? (same as in video)

Based on this todo list I worked on my onshape file, where I couldn't do step 1 (I tried but the file was a scan and sucked badly) and went onto step 2. I learned how to do configurations with specific dimensions which I hadn't done before in onshape (only had done a list dropdown) and applied that to the square beam file. 

From there I decided that I am GOING to keep the rectangular shape (for now) as it is the most simple and I don't have the resources to make a really cool custom shaped frame due to cost. This shape is also heavily dependent on cost as well, because if I can get a lower cost frame or lets say use a harbor freight lifting cart as the frame then I will scrap this.

I tried to get some parts of the kart imported but I could only find the cad for the wheels as I priotitized prime shipping/cost for most parts. May have to change parts to some aliexpress ones if there are cad files or documentation. I did not get to task 5 or 6 as it is pretty late but will get on those ASAP tmrw.

I had a lot of great ideas of next steps when designing even though I got stuck at some points, and the main ones are to make a custom corner magnet holder to use when welding square beams (saw it in the video but thought it would be nice to make) and also I am thinking of 3d printing or laser cutting the seat and making it adjustable using some extrusion and 3d printed parts for different people (will research adjusting seats tmrw)

Overall great CAD session and there is way more incoming (I may end up having to switch some of these parts to ones I can CAD out as well). The CAD sadly looks so basic but figuring it out was a pain.

 May 26: Research/Brainstorm with Coach

Though we actually talked for an hours, I am not going to add it fully because the conversation was interuptted a few times and I'm not even sure stuff like this should be tracked (will ask later). School was back in today so I went over to my FTC coach/engineering professor who has made gokarts before and has lots of welding experience. I showed him my current design and he gave me a lot of good tips. Mainly ideas on how to mount the square beams without welding, however he strongly was opposed to me using 3d prints. He said it was a bad idea and due to the simplicity of the frame we could easily weld it without much effort. I also got to ask him about my motor choice/what he thought about the usage of aluminum extrusion to support the seat (as I want to save cost/steel extrusion as much as possible) and he approved both saying that they were simple and the aluminum should be enough. Going to do a few lapses where I will hopefully start the seat design today.
